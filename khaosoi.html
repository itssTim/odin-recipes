Khao Soi Recipe

Description

Khao Soi is a popular and flavorful dish from northern Thailand, particularly known in Chiang Mai. It's a rich, creamy coconut curry soup made with egg noodles, often served with a combination of tender chicken, beef, or pork. The dish is typically garnished with crispy fried noodles, pickled mustard greens, shallots, and a squeeze of lime, creating a perfect balance of textures and flavors. Its unique combination of spicy, savory, and slightly sweet elements makes it a beloved comfort food, reflecting the cultural influences of both Thai and Burmese cuisines. Khao Soi is often enjoyed with a side of chili oil or additional condiments for extra heat.

Ingredients

  • 2 pods black cardamom
  • 2 1.5 tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 5g fresh tumeric
  • 1/2 cup shallots
  • 0.75 oz mild chilies
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1.5 cups coconut milk
  • 8 chicken drumsticks
  • 3 cups water
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon black soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar
  • 12 oz flat fresh egg noodles
  • 1 lime
  • 1/4 cup chopped red onions
  • 1/2 cup chopped pickled mustard greens
  • Fried chili flakes

Steps

Curry Paste

  1. Smash cardamom pods, remove seeds, and toast with coriander seeds in a dry skillet over high heat until darkened. Set aside.
  2. Sear ginger and turmeric slices in the skillet for 2-3 minutes until charred, then remove.
  3. Char sliced shallots in the skillet for a few seconds, stir, and remove.
  4. Cut dried chilies into chunks, remove seeds, and grind with cardamom and coriander seeds into a powder. Set aside.
  5. Pound ginger, turmeric, shallots, and coarse salt into a fine paste in a mortar. Add ground spices and mix.

Curry Broth

  1. In a pot, bring ½ cup coconut milk to a boil. Stir in curry paste, cook until thickened and oil sizzles. Add remaining coconut milk and water, soy sauces, sugar, and chicken. Simmer on low for 40 minutes until chicken is tender.
  2. Taste and adjust seasoning with fish sauce or salt, ensuring the broth is bold for the noodles.

Fried Components

  1. Loosen 3 oz noodles and cut into 4-inch pieces. Heat 2 inches of oil to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Fry a small handful of noodles at a time until golden and bubbling slows. Drain on paper towels. Repeat with the rest.
  3. Heat chili flakes in oil over medium-low heat until darkened and aromatic. Transfer to a bowl to stop cooking.

Assembly

  1. Boil water and cook noodles per package instructions. Drain, toss with a tablespoon of oil.
  2. Divide noodles into bowls, add chicken, pour curry broth over, and drizzle with coconut milk.
  3. Top with crispy noodles and condiments. Serve immediately.
