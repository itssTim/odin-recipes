Oyakodon Recipe

Description

Oyakodon is a comforting Japanese rice bowl dish featuring tender chicken, onions, and eggs simmered in a savory-sweet broth made from soy sauce, mirin, and dashi. The ingredients are cooked together until the chicken is juicy and the eggs are slightly soft and velvety, creating a harmonious blend of flavors and textures. "Oyakodon" translates to "parent-and-child bowl," referencing the use of both chicken (the "parent") and eggs (the "child"). Served over a steaming bowl of white rice, it’s a simple yet satisfying meal, beloved for its warmth and homey feel.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 onion
  • 10 oz boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 1 tablespoon sake
  • 3-4 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup dash
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons mirin
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 2 servings cooked rice
  • 4 sprigs green onion or scallion

Steps

Seasoning Preparation

  1. In a bowl mix 1/2 cup dashi, 2 tablespoons soy sauce, 2 tablespoons mirin, and 2 teaspoon sugar. Stirring until dissolved

Ingredient prep

  1. Thinly slice 1/2 onion
  2. Chop 4 sprigs green onion or scallion
  3. Trim fat and connective tissue from 10 oz boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  4. Slice chicken along the grain into 1 inch strips, then cut diagonally against the grain into 1 inch pieces (sogigiri style) for even cooking
  5. Place chicken in a bowl, sprinkle with 1 Tbsp sake, and let sit for 5 minutes
  6. Crack 3–4 large eggs into a bowl. Use chopsticks to cut through the whites 5–6 times—don’t fully mix. Maintain a marbled look with distinct white and yellow parts.

Cooking Oyakodon

  1. With the heat off, layer onions in a medium pan. Pour in the seasoning—just enough to cover the onions
  2. Bring to a simmer over medium heat. Add chicken on top
  3. Distribute evenly. Once simmering again, reduce heat to medium low. Cook uncovered for 5 minutes, flipping chicken halfway
  4. Turn heat to medium. Once bubbling, drizzle in 2/3 of the eggs in a circular motion, avoiding pan edges. Add more whites at this stage
  5. If egg moves to the pan edges, gently move it back to the center. Cover if needed to help the whites set
  6. When eggs are mostly set but still runny, add the remaining one-third. Top with green onion or scallion and cook to desired doneness
  7. Serve over steamed rice, spooning chicken and egg mixture on top with some sauce
