Kimbap Recipe

Description

Kimbap is a popular Korean dish made of steamed rice and assorted fillings such as vegetables, eggs, pickled radish, and meat or seafood, all rolled in sheets of seaweed (gim) and sliced into bite-sized pieces. Often compared to sushi rolls, kimbap is distinct in its use of sesame oil–flavored rice instead of vinegar and typically includes cooked rather than raw ingredients. It’s a convenient, colorful, and nutritious meal commonly enjoyed as a picnic food, lunchbox staple, or street snack in Korea.

Steps

Ingredients

  • 4 dried seaweed sheets, also known as sushi nori
  • 120g spinach
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 carrot
  • 2 to 4 imitation crab sticks
  • 4 sticks BBQ Kimbap ham
  • 4 yellow picked radish
  • 12 strips seasoned edible burdock root
  • 2 1/2 cups steamed rice
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 3/8 teaspoon fine sea salt

Steps

  1. Rinse spinach and parboil for 30 seconds to 1 minute. Rinse under cold water, squeeze out excess moisture, and season with 1/8 tsp salt and ½ Tbsp sesame oil. Mix well
  2. Cook a beaten egg in a lightly oiled pan over medium heat until set on both sides. Slice into long strips
  3. Lightly sauté carrots, crab sticks, and kimbap ham separately over medium heat for about 1 minute each
  4. Season cooked rice with ½ Tbsp sesame oil and ¼ tsp sea salt (adjust to taste), and mix well
  5. Set up a rolling station with all prepared ingredients. Place a sheet of dried seaweed (shiny side down) on a bamboo mat. Spread rice thinly over the lower 2/3 of the seaweed, leaving a 2-inch gap at the top
  6. Arrange filling: yellow pickled radish, 3 burdock strips, 1 strip each of ham and crab stick, followed by egg strips, carrots, and spinach
  7. Roll tightly. If the seaweed doesn’t seal, dab water or a few rice grains on the edge.Repeat with remaining ingredients
  8. Brush finished rolls with 1 Tbsp sesame oil, slice into bite-sized pieces, and serve
